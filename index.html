The thesis, stated plainly

Measure stress. Then optimise against it.

The frantic, zig-zagging gather and the patient, wide arc both end with sheep in the fold — but one spikes every animal's arousal and the other barely registers.

01 · MANY WAYS TO WIN

Success isn't unique

For any herding goal, many policies pen the flock. They differ enormously in welfare cost — the job gets done either way, but not equally kindly.

02 · COST IS MEASURABLE

Stress has a number

Flock stress is measurable from ear angle — a posture the affective-state literature ties to arousal (Reefmann 2009, Boissy 2011). That's what SamSeesSheep reads off real video.

03 · THEREFORE OPTIMISABLE

RL should find the gentle one

If the cost is measurable, it's optimisable. A learning agent should sit on a better speed-vs-stress frontier than naïve driving — without failing the job.

Where this came from. SamSeesSheep answers a measurement question — how stressed is this sheep, right now? Once you can measure it, a control question won't leave you alone: can I optimise against it, and will a learning agent discover handling gentler than the textbook heuristic? shepherd·gym is the testbed for that. It's the next step in doing applied AI on my own flock.

How it works

A stress-aware reward loop

A pure-numpy, Gymnasium-compatible, PufferLib-ready environment. The policy sees only what's sensable — positions, velocities, flock geometry, and a noisy ear-angle observable (the sim analogue of SamSeesSheep). The critic gets the true latent arousal; the reward integrates real flock stress. That asymmetry mirrors deployment: at run time you only have the noisy CV estimate.

30-SEED EVAL · BEST CHECKPOINT
PPO (welfare-weighted) flank · Strömbom expert ablations

The welfare weight r_arousal traces a real speed-vs-stress frontier. The sweet spot (r=0.25) herds almost as well as the expert at less than half the stress — and beats the no-welfare ablation (r=0) on both axes, because pressing too hard scatters the flock. Push the penalty too far (r=0.5) and it goes over-passive; success drops.

why it worked → PPO from scratch couldn't crack the task (~16%, flat). Behaviour-cloning the flank expert past the exploration wall, then fine-tuning for welfare, was the unlock.

⚠ honest caveats — I'd rather be honest than oversell
  • Thin & noisy. 30-seed eval (±~9%), one seed per welfare weight, three points only.
  • Unstable fine-tuning. Runs peak then drift — these are best-checkpoint policies (what you'd deploy), not converged.
  • Not yet data-calibrated. The arousal model is literature-grounded (flight-zone), not fit to real ear-angle data yet. This is a methods demonstration, not a claim about real Katahdins. Making it empirical is the whole roadmap.

What you'll build

A real welfare-aware shepherd, from baselines to a policy on the Pareto front — each level unlocked by beating a benchmark.

  1. 01The environment & the welfare reward — obs, actions, and why arousal is in the reward.
  2. 02Baselines — random, greedy, and the Strömbom flank expert. Establish the bar.
  3. 03The exploration wall — why PPO from scratch flatlines at ~16%.
  4. 04Behaviour cloning — warm-start from the expert to get past it.
  5. 05Reward shaping & the frontier — tune r_arousal; find the speed-vs-stress sweet spot.
  6. 06Asymmetric actor–critic — a privileged critic, a deployable policy.
  7. 07Evaluation — held-out folds, seeds, and reading a Pareto front honestly.
